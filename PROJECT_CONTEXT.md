## Startup Plan

App name:
- ClipForge AI

One-line idea:
- ClipForge AI turns a topic, script, article, product page, or voice note into a finished short video for TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, and ads.

Core positioning:
- Do not compete as just another AI video generator.
- Build a workflow app that turns ideas into finished short videos faster.

Market notes:
- AI video demand is growing because creators and businesses need more video while editing still takes time.
- Strong competitors include Runway, Synthesia, Pika, and Google Veo.
- The gap is workflow: most tools generate clips, but do not fully help users plan, write, edit, brand, format, publish, and improve videos in one flow.

Product idea:
- The app should act like an AI video production assistant.
- Inputs can include a topic, product link, script, long video, voice note, blog post, school project, or business idea.
- Outputs can include a hook, script, storyboard, scenes, voiceover, captions, background music, stock or AI clips, brand colors, final export, title, description, and platform-specific versions.

Example:
- Input: "Make a 30-second video ad for a hoodie brand."
- Output: hook options, short script, five-scene storyboard, AI voiceover, vertical video, captions, music, CTA, TikTok caption, and YouTube Shorts title.

Target users:
- Small business owners
- TikTok creators
- YouTubers
- Students
- Real estate agents
- Coaches
- Podcasters
- Online sellers

Best first niche:
- Small businesses that need short ad videos but do not have editors.

Reason:
- They have a clear pain, they need videos to sell, and they pay when the tool saves time.

Common responses:
- If the video looks wrong, change style, audience, or scene instructions and describe the product, viewer, tone, and length more clearly.
- If the voiceover sounds off, regenerate only the voice track.
- If the user needs a business video, direct them to a product ad template and tone options.
- Free exports can include a watermark; paid plans remove it.

## Risks And Fixes

High AI costs:
- Limit free usage
- Use credits
- Make users approve scripts before generating video

Generic videos:
- Add brand kits, custom templates, product photos, and better scene control

Copyright issues:
- Use licensed stock media, safe music, and AI content labels

Competition:
- Focus on workflow, templates, niche users, and speed

Misuse:
- Add moderation, watermarking, and rules against scams, impersonation, and harmful content

Best version of the app:
- A user with no editing skill answers five questions:
- What is the video about?
- Who is watching?
- What platform is it for?
- What style do you want?
- What action should viewers take?
- Then the app makes the full video.

## Why Random Black-And-White Clips Happened

Problem summary:
- Search logic was too loose.
- Internet Archive searches were too broad.
- Broad words like `speech`, `crowd`, `history`, `trial`, or `people` pulled unrelated old footage.
- The app labeled clips as exact-topic matches too easily.
- It fell back to related B-roll too quickly.

Better rule set:
- Exact clips should only show when the title, tags, or source text include the subject.
- If there is no exact legal clip, the app should say so.
- Related B-roll should be separate and clearly labeled.
- Internet Archive should be off by default unless the user explicitly wants archive footage.
- Black-and-white footage should only show for archive or history topics.
- Stock B-roll should prefer Pixabay or Pexels.

## v15 Fix Notes

The stricter `v15` version was described as:
- Exact clips must contain the subject
- Related clips must match the scene words
- Random archive footage is off
- Internet Archive is no longer used for random fallback
- Results are split into `EXACT TOPIC` and `RELATED B-ROLL`
